# Gallery Wall Ruleset Research

This app uses a practical ruleset rather than a single aesthetic formula.

## Default Placement Rules

- Treat the centerline of a single artwork or complete arrangement as roughly 57 inches from the floor.
- Use consistent spacing between frames. Two inches is a common minimum; two to four inches is a reliable gallery-wall range; gaps larger than six to eight inches can make a grouping feel disconnected.
- Prefer a grid when pieces share the same size or intentionally matching frames.
- Prefer an organic layout when pieces vary in size; start with a strong central anchor and build outward while keeping spacing consistent.
- Keep healthy margin around the complete grouping so art does not feel cramped against wall boundaries.
- Never allow pieces to overlap or touch.
- Do not place one piece across a wall corner or section boundary in the MVP.

## Sources

- Better Homes & Gardens, "How to Hang a Gallery Wall in 5 Easy Steps": 57-inch centerline, planning the arrangement, and above-furniture spacing guidance.
- Real Simple, "The Best Way to Hang Pictures": painter's tape planning, about two inches between frames, 57-inch centerline, and hardware considerations.
- House Beautiful, "The 9 Most Common Gallery Wall Mistakes": two to four inch spacing, avoid large disconnected gaps, maintain cohesion, and use 70-75 percent wall fill as a scale guideline.
- Good Housekeeping, "Are Grid Walls About to Be Everywhere?": grid layouts work best with repeated frame size, equidistant spacing, and visual rhythm.
